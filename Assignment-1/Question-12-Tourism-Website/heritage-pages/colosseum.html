Colosseum

The Colosseum, also known as the Flavian Amphitheatre, is an ancient amphitheater located in the center of Rome. It is one of the greatest works of Roman engineering and architecture. Built between 70 and 80 AD, the Colosseum could hold up to 80,000 spectators and was used for gladiatorial contests, public spectacles, and other entertainment events.

The Colosseum is an iconic symbol of the Roman Empire and has been a major tourist attraction for centuries. Despite the damage caused by earthquakes and stone robbers, it remains a prominent and well-preserved piece of ancient Roman history.
