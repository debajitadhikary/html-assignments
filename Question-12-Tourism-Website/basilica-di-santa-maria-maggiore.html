Basilica di Santa Maria Maggiore

The Basilica di Santa Maria Maggiore is one of the four major basilicas of Rome, and it is the largest church dedicated to the Virgin Mary in the city. Located on the Esquiline Hill, this basilica is renowned for its stunning mosaics, which date back to the 5th century, and its grandiose interior.

Built during the reign of Pope Sixtus III, the basilica features a striking blend of early Christian and Baroque architectural styles. The Basilica di Santa Maria Maggiore holds significant historical and religious importance and remains a key destination for visitors exploring Rome's rich heritage.
