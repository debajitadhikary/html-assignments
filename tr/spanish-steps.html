Spanish Steps

The Spanish Steps, or Scalinata di Trinità dei Monti, is a monumental stairway of 135 steps located in Piazza di Spagna in Rome. The steps were built in 1723-1725 and were designed to connect the Spanish Embassy and the Trinità dei Monti church with the Piazza di Spagna below.

The Spanish Steps are a popular tourist attraction and a famous meeting point in Rome. The steps are renowned for their beautiful architectural design and their vibrant surroundings, which include elegant shops, cafes, and the stunning Fountain of the Barcaccia at the base of the stairs.
